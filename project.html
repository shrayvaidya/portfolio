Animations

Describe each of the four GIFs

My first gif is a sunrise, using ellipses for clouds and a modified version of the looping sun from Dr. Kate's own example. I also made the background change color from dark red to a light yellow over the duration of the loop, to simulate how the sky changes color as the sun rises. My second gif is a simple city scene at night. I used two circles to give the effect of a cresent moon in the sky, and I used rectangles to draw out the buildings, their windows, and the ground. I set the color of the windows to fade from bright yellow to dull gray to simulate people turning their lights on and off. My third gif is a galaxy with some planets orbiting a sun. I followed an example I found online to simulate the stars in the background, and then drew some circles and rings for the planets. Finally, I used some simple polar math to animate the planets' orbit. My fourth gid is meant to simulate an atom with electrons circling it, although I wasn't quite able to get the electrons to line up with their paths perfectly. I used a similar approach with polar looping as I did in the galaxy gif.

What P5 method or technique did you enjoy using the most? What was interesting to you about it?

I enjoyed using the polar looping techniques, since I think revolving objects tend to look really fluid and seamless.

Did you find any P5 functionality that wasn't covered in class?

I followed a tutorial for drawing the stars in the background of my galaxy gif that defined and used a separate Star class, which I thought was really interesting. I also experimented a little bit with the clear and frameRate functions, which I don't recall covering in class.

How did you get your looping gif to loop smoothly? What was hard or easy about that techinque?

I followed the polar looping approach, using this.loopTime as a set variable that I could then create a percentage off of using t. I manipulated that percentage with radians to get the planets to complete one full circle around the sun in the time it took the gif to complete one loop.
